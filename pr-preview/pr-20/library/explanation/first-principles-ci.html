First Principles of Collaborative Intelligence

Evolving document. Initial draft.

1. Don’t Fool Yourself

“The first principle is that you must not fool yourself — and you are the easiest person to fool.” — Richard Feynman

2. Humility About Your Skull

“The competent programmer is fully aware of the strictly limited size of his own skull.” — Edsger Dijkstra

3. The Whole is Other Than the Sum

“The whole is other than the sum of its parts.” — Kurt Koffka

4. Only Variety Absorbs Variety

“Only variety can absorb variety.” — W. Ross Ashby

5. Understand Before Changing

“If you don’t see the use of it, I certainly won’t let you clear it away.” — G.K. Chesterton

6. Antifragility Over Robustness

“Antifragility is beyond resilience. The resilient resists shocks and stays the same; the antifragile gets better.” — Nassim Taleb

7. Fuzzy Over False Precision

“As complexity rises, precise statements lose meaning and meaningful statements lose precision.” — Lotfi Zadeh
