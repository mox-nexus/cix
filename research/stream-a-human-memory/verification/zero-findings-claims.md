## Finding 1.4: Insight CAN be reported reliably post-hoc

### beeman2004:c5 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: Subjects' post-hoc self-report of insight vs non-insight correlated with objective neural differences.
SOURCE: "Subjects solved verbal problems, and after each correct solution indicated whether they solved with or without insight. We observed two objective neural correlates of insight."
VERIFICATION: The neural correlates (RH aSTG fMRI, gamma burst EEG) were identified BY sorting trials according to self-report. Self-report was the basis for the neural discovery. Claim accurately represents this relationship.

### metcalfewiebe1987:c7 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: In Metcalfe 1986b, warmth ratings remained low/flat until solution on insight problems, then jumped (step-function).
SOURCE (from 1987 paper introduction): "If the problems were solved by what subjectively is a sudden flash of insight, one would expect that the warmth ratings would be fairly low and constant until solution, at which point they would jump to a high value. This is what was found in the experiment."
VERIFICATION: Quote is verbatim from the 1987 paper reporting 1986b results. Step-function pattern confirmed. (Same source-attribution note as metcalfewiebe1987:c8 — primary source is 1986b.)

## Finding 1.5: Positive affect facilitates insight

### subramaniam2008:c1 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: Participants higher in positive mood solved more problems, specifically more with insight.
SOURCE: "Participants higher in positive mood solved more problems, and specifically more with insight, compared with participants lower in positive mood."
VERIFICATION: Verbatim match.

### subramaniam2008:c2 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: Positive mood modulated preparatory brain activity before each problem, biasing toward insight.
SOURCE: "Positive mood... was associated with changes in brain activity during a preparatory interval preceding each solved problem; modulation of preparatory activity in several areas biased people to solve either with insight or analytically."
VERIFICATION: Accurate. Extraction omits the anxiety mention ("and to a lesser extent and in the opposite direction, anxiety") — not a distortion, just a scope narrowing to the positive-mood finding.

### subramaniam2008:c3 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: ACC showed sensitivity to both mood and insight; positive mood alters preparatory ACC activity.
SOURCE: "the ACC showed sensitivity to both mood and insight, demonstrating that positive mood alters preparatory activity in ACC, biasing participants to engage in processing conducive to insight solving."
VERIFICATION: Near-verbatim.

## Finding 4.3: Delayed reflection improves metacognitive accuracy

### thiede2003:c2 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: Delayed keyword generation produced greater monitoring accuracy than immediate or none.
SOURCE: "Accuracy was greater for a group that wrote keywords after a delay (delayed-keyword group) than for a group that wrote keywords immediately after reading (immediatekeyword group) and a group that did not write keywords (no-keyword group)."
VERIFICATION: Accurately captures the three-condition comparison.

### thiede2003:c3 — VERIFIED
CLAIM: Superior monitoring accuracy → more effective study regulation → greater comprehension.
SOURCE: "The superior monitoring accuracy produced more effective regulation of study. Differences in monitoring accuracy and regulation of study, in turn, produced greater overall test performance (reading comprehension)"
VERIFICATION: Accurately captures the causal chain as described by authors.
