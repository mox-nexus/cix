## Extraction Notes

- c10 is the highest-relevance claim for the fog-match hypothesis. "Cognitively present retrieval information" is the phrase most directly aligned with Yash's intuition: retrieval is driven not by a stored key alone but by the current cognitive state jointly with the stored trace. The word "cognitively present" marks this as Tulving's formal analog of the fog-match idea.
- "Tentative acceptance" (c3) is a claim, not a hedge to skip. This is Tulving's explicit epistemic stance — the distinction is a working hypothesis.
- c1 and c2 split from one sentence: "functionally separate" and "closely interacting" are two independent properties.
- c13 is a compound claim kept unsplit because the synergistic ecphory model is defined by ALL its connecting properties. Splitting would lose the model's coherence.
- 7 additional structural claims (about Tulving 1972, possible extensions, empirical vs theoretical reasons) were condensed or omitted relative to full decomposition to keep extraction focused on the load-bearing theoretical claims. A full-text extraction would yield substantially more.
