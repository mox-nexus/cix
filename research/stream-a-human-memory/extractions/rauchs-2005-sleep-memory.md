# Extraction: Rauchs et al 2005 — Sleep and Memory Systems

## Atomic Claims

### rauchs2005:c1
- **Claim:** Sleep is involved in the off-line reprocessing of recently-acquired memories.
- **Quote:** "Several lines of evidence support the hypothesis that sleep is involved in the off-line reprocessing of recently-acquired memories."
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### rauchs2005:c2
- **Claim:** All four long-term memory systems (procedural, perceptual representation, semantic, and episodic per Tulving's SPI model) benefit from either NREM, REM, or both sleep stages.
- **Quote:** "the four long-term memory systems (procedural memory, perceptual representation system, semantic and episodic memory, according to Tulving's SPI model) benefit either from non-rapid eye movement (NREM)... or rapid eye movement (REM) sleep, or from both sleep stages."
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### rauchs2005:c3
- **Claim:** Tulving's SPI classification (procedural, perceptual, semantic, episodic) is more pertinent than the declarative/non-declarative dichotomy for understanding sleep's role in memory.
- **Quote:** "Tulving's classification of memory systems appears more pertinent than the declarative/non-declarative dichotomy when it comes to understanding the role of sleep in memory."
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0
- **Note:** Direct tension with Squire's declarative/procedural taxonomy. Different cuts of the memory system taxonomy are better for different purposes.

### rauchs2005:c4
- **Claim:** Episodic and semantic memory (both declarative) rely on different sleep stages, undermining the declarative/non-declarative boundary for sleep research.
- **Quote:** "this model allows us to resolve several contradictions, notably the fact that episodic and semantic memory... appear to rely on different sleep stages."
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### rauchs2005:c5
- **Claim:** Different types of implicit/non-declarative skills (perceptual-motor, sensory-perceptual, cognitive) may not benefit from the same sleep stages, undermining the unitary non-declarative category.
- **Quote:** "various types of skills... and priming effects, subserved by different brain structures but all designated by the generic term of implicit or non-declarative memory, may not benefit from the same sleep stages."
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0
