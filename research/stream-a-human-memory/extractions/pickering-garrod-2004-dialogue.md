# Extraction: Pickering & Garrod 2004 — Psychology of Dialogue

## Atomic Claims

### pickering2004:c1
- **Claim:** The most natural and basic form of language use is dialogue, not monologue; traditional language processing accounts derived from monologue may offer limited theories of language processing in general.
- **Quote:** "the most natural and basic form of language use is dialogue. As a result, these accounts may only offer limited theories of the mechanisms that underlie language processing in general."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2

### pickering2004:c2
- **Claim:** In dialogue, linguistic representations become aligned at many levels between interlocutors, through a largely automatic process (interactive alignment).
- **Quote:** "in dialogue, the linguistic representations employed by the interlocutors become aligned at many levels, as a result of a largely automatic process."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2
- **Note:** DIRECTLY RELEVANT to memex's conversation domain. If conversational memory is about shared representations that ALIGN during dialogue, then what gets stored is not just individual utterances but a jointly constructed alignment state.

### pickering2004:c3

- **Quote:** "This process greatly simplifies production and comprehension in dialogue."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2
