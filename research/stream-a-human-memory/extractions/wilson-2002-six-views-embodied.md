### wilson2002:c6
- **Claim:** Wilson distinguishes two types of off-loading: long-term archival (reference books, calendars, computer files — to avoid memorizing) and situated (to avoid encoding/holding in STM).
- **Quote:** "The environment can also be used as a long-term archive, as in the use of reference books, appointment calendars, and computer files. This can be thought of as off-loading to avoid memorizing"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2
- **Note:** DIRECTLY describes memex's purpose: archival off-loading. Wilson herself considers this the LESS theoretically interesting type, but it's exactly what memex is.

### wilson2002:c7

### wilson2002:c9

### wilson2002:c14

- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### wilson2002:c15
- **Claim:** Episodic memory retrieval has "reliving" phenomenology with visual, kinesthetic, and spatial impressions; this quality diminishes as memories crystallize through retelling into semantic form.
- **Quote:** "recalling an episodic memory has a quality of 'reliving,' with all the attendant visual, kinesthetic, and spatial impressions. This is especially true when memories are fresh, before they have become crystallized by retelling"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3
- **Note:** Directly relevant to Yash's "idea solidification" question: fresh memories are embodied reliving; solidified ones become semantic. The trajectory FROM reliving TO crystallized-semantic IS the solidification process.

### wilson2002:c16

## Extraction Notes

- Full-text extraction. 44 claims in raw agent output; condensed to 20 highest-impact.
- c6 (archival off-loading) directly describes memex's purpose but Wilson considers it LESS theoretically interesting than situated off-loading. This is noteworthy — memex operates in the theoretically "boring" quadrant of embodied cognition. The interesting question is whether memex can make archival off-loading theoretically interesting by connecting it to Claim 6 (off-line body-based cognition).
- c11-c12 (purpose-neutral storage) is architecturally important: if human memory stores broadly rather than indexing to anticipated use, memex should too. This argues against narrow retrieval-optimized storage and toward rich, broad encoding.
- c15 (reliving → crystallization) directly maps to Yash's "idea solidification" trajectory: fresh episodic memories are embodied simulation; retelling crystallizes them into semantic representations. The solidification process IS the transition from body-based reliving to abstract semantic knowledge.
