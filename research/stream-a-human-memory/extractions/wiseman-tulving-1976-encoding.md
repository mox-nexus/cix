### wiseman1976:c2

### wiseman1976:c3
- **Claim:** Recall superiority is a sufficient but not necessary condition for recognition failure of recallable words — extending the generality of encoding specificity.
- **Quote:** "These results extend the generality of encoding specificity and suggest that although recall superiority is a sufficient condition for recognition failure of recallable words it is not a necessary condition."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

### wiseman1976:c4
- **Claim:** Under certain conditions, people recall learned words that they fail to recognize — the phenomenon of recognition failure of recallable words.
- **Quote:** "people do recall learned list words that they fail to recognize."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3
- **Note:** Core encoding specificity paradox: the "right" retrieval cue (one that was present at encoding) can succeed where a copy of the item itself fails. Directly supports the fog-match hypothesis.
