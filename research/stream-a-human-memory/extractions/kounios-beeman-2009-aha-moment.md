# Extraction: Kounios & Beeman 2009 — The Aha! Moment

## Atomic Claims

### kounios-beeman-2009:c1
- **Claim:** The term "insight" (the "Aha! moment") is defined as a sudden comprehension that solves a problem, reinterprets a situation, explains a joke, or resolves an ambiguous percept.
- **Quote:** "A sudden comprehension that solves a problem, reinterprets a situation, explains a joke, or resolves an ambiguous percept is called an insight (i.e., the 'Aha! moment')."

## Extraction Notes

- c6 and c7 split: "sudden" and "can seem disconnected from preceding thought" are distinct phenomenological descriptors with different experimental implications (suddenness predicts sharp behavioral onset; disconnectedness predicts poor introspective access to pre-insight cognition).
- c8 and c9 split: "culmination of a series of brain states" (c8) and "operating at different time scales" (c9) are logically independent: a series could operate at a single time scale.
- c10 assigned Tier 3 despite Tier-1 source because the claim is inferential ("suggests") not demonstrated. Source tier does not elevate the logical status of a hedge claim.
- c7 has secondary RQ4 relevance: if introspective access to the pre-insight period is limited, then user self-report about WHEN the click happened may be unreliable as a timestamp.
- Abstract-only limitation: specific EEG signatures (gamma-band bursts), specific fMRI activations (right anterior temporal lobe), specific time windows, and specific studies composing "a series of studies" are in the body. Recommend full-text upgrade in batch 2.
