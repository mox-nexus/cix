# Extraction: Friston et al 2017 — Active Inference, Curiosity and Insight

## Atomic Claims

### friston2017:c1
- **Claim:** Curiosity and insight can be formally modeled as active (Bayesian) inference — minimizing expected variational free energy leads to active sampling of novel contingencies.
- **Quote:** "This article offers a formal account of curiosity and insight in terms of active (Bayesian) inference."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1
- **Note:** NOVELTY. Reframes insight within the free-energy principle. Insight is not a special mechanism but a consequence of the brain's general inference machinery.

### friston2017:c2
- **Claim:** Epistemic behavior (curious sampling) closes explanatory gaps in generative models, reducing uncertainty and satisfying curiosity.
- **Quote:** "This epistemic behavior closes explanatory gaps in generative models of the world, thereby reducing uncertainty and satisfying curiosity."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1, RQ3

### friston2017:c3
- **Claim:** Abductive processes emerge when agents test plausible hypotheses about symmetries (invariances or rules) in their generative models — this is Bayesian model reduction.
- **Quote:** "abductive processes emerge when agents test plausible hypotheses about symmetries (i.e., invariances or rules) in their generative models."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1

### friston2017:c4
- **Claim:** Bayesian model reduction (the process producing "aha" moments) evinces mechanisms associated with sleep.
- **Quote:** "The ensuing Bayesian model reduction evinces mechanisms associated with sleep and has all the hallmarks of 'aha' moments."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1, RQ0
- **Note:** Links insight to sleep-dependent consolidation. If "aha" moments share machinery with sleep, then the reconsolidation finding (Nader 2009) is relevant: sleep-like processes may reopen and restructure models.

### friston2017:c5
- **Claim:** People attain insight using just a handful of observations solicited through curious behavior, in contrast to deep learning which requires large datasets.
- **Quote:** "we focus on how people attain insight and understanding using just a handful of observations, which are solicited through curious behavior."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1, bridge
- **Note:** Bridge to Stream B: human insight is sample-efficient where current ML is data-hungry. This is a structural difference between human and LLM memory/learning.
