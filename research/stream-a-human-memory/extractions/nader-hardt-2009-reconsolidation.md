# Extraction: Nader & Hardt 2009 — Reconsolidation

## Atomic Claims

### nader2009:c1
- **Claim:** Consolidated long-term memories can re-enter states of transient instability following reactivation, and must again stabilize in order to persist.
- **Quote:** "Consolidated memories can re-enter states of transient instability following reactivation, from which they must again stabilize in order to persist"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ2, RQ3

### nader2009:c2
- **Claim:** Retrieval from long-term memory can induce states of instability requiring additional stabilization (reconsolidation), fundamentally challenging the classical linear consolidation model.
- **Quote:** "The finding that retrieval from LTM can again induce states of instability requiring additional stabilization (reconsolidation) fundamentally challenged this linear model."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ3

### nader2009:c3
- **Claim:** Memory exists in two states: a labile state susceptible to enhancement or impairment, and a stable (consolidated) state insensitive to amnesic treatments.
- **Quote:** "memory exists in two states: a labile state, in which it is susceptible to enhancement or impairment; and a stable state, in which memory is insensitive to these treatments"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ2

### nader2009:c4
- **Claim:** Memory permanence requires continuous molecular maintenance (PKMzeta phosphorylation); LTM can be abolished at any time by disrupting maintenance without reactivation.
- **Quote:** "the molecular substrate of LTM requires continuous phosphorylation by protein kinase Mzeta (PKMzeta)"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2

### nader2009:c5
- **Claim:** Memories are not consolidated just once; they can return to a labile state and require reconsolidation each time they are reactivated.
- **Quote:** "memories are not consolidated, or stabilized, just once: they can return to a labile state and need to be reconsolidated, or restabilized, when reactivated."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ2, RQ3
- **Note:** ARCHITECTURALLY LOAD-BEARING for memex. Every recall is also a potential modification event. The "stored unit" is rewritten on each retrieval.

### nader2009:c6
- **Claim:** Reactivation-induced instability is transient: delaying post-reactivation amnesic treatment by 6 hours has no effect on memory.
- **Quote:** "if the post-reactivation infusion was delayed by 6 h it had no effect, demonstrating that the reactivation-induced instability was transient."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### nader2009:c7
- **Claim:** Reactivation — not the amnesic agent alone — is what renders a consolidated memory labile again.
- **Quote:** "animals that were not reminded of the conditioning session before anisomycin infusions had intact memory, showing that it was indeed reactivation that made this consolidated memory labile again."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

### nader2009:c8
- **Claim:** Memory can be enhanced, impaired, or interfered with by new learning only during the reconsolidation phase; these treatments are ineffective once reconsolidation is complete.
- **Quote:** "only during the reconsolidation phase can memory be enhanced, impaired by amnesic treatments or interfered with by new learning. These treatments are ineffective when reconsolidation is complete."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### nader2009:c9
- **Claim:** Reconsolidation has been demonstrated across a variety of tasks, amnesic treatments, and species, establishing it as a fundamental mnemonic process.
- **Quote:** "it has been shown to occur across a variety of tasks, amnesic treatments and species. Given that evidence for reconsolidation has been obtained from a wide range of manipulations, it clearly represents a fundamental mnemonic process."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### nader2009:c10
- **Claim:** Evidence for reconsolidation exists at behavioural, physiological, and molecular levels of analysis.
- **Quote:** "striking evidence for the existence of a post-reactivation transient period of memory plasticity — that is, memory reconsolidation — on behavioural, physiological and molecular levels of analysis."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### nader2009:c11
- **Claim:** Boundary conditions for reconsolidation include extinction consolidation, memory age, predictability of the reactivation stimulus, and training intensity.
- **Quote:** "Several boundary conditions have been proposed, such as extinction consolidation, memory age, predictability of the reactivation stimulus and training intensity."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ3

### nader2009:c12
- **Claim:** In human episodic memory, reactivation-dependent interference effects occur only when subjects are exposed to interfering material in the same spatial environment as original learning.
- **Quote:** "reactivation-dependent interference effects in consolidated episodic memory were found only when human subjects were exposed to the interfering material in the same environment in which the original learning took place."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3
- **Note:** Directly relevant to the fog-match hypothesis: context reinstatement is required for reconsolidation to fire.

### nader2009:c13
- **Claim:** Both consolidation and reconsolidation are characterized by a transient instability period; the difference is only the initiating event (acquisition vs. reactivation).
- **Quote:** "In the case of consolidation, this phase is initiated after acquisition of new information; in the case of reconsolidation, it is initiated after reactivation of an existing, consolidated memory."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ2

### nader2009:c14
- **Claim:** Post-reactivation amnesic treatments that reverse the cellular and molecular correlates of LTM represent failure of memory re-storage, not mere retrieval impairment.
- **Quote:** "post-reactivation amnesic treatments reverse cellular and molecular correlates of LTM should consequently be taken as evidence that post-reactivation memory impairments represent a failure in memory re-storage."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### nader2009:c15
- **Claim:** Reconsolidation is completed faster than consolidation, and their dose-response curves are not identical.
- **Quote:** "reconsolidation is completed faster than consolidation, and their dose-response curves are not identical."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### nader2009:c16
- **Claim:** Protein degradation via the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway is required for a consolidated memory to become labile again following reactivation.
- **Quote:** "protein degradation by the ubiquitin-proteasome pathway is required in order for a consolidated memory to become labile again."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### nader2009:c17
- **Claim:** Reactivation of a consolidated memory initiates a consolidation-like, time-dependent process sharing many properties with the state following initial learning.
- **Quote:** "reactivation of a consolidated memory induces a time-dependent memory state that shares many properties with the state that follows initial learning."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0, RQ3

### nader2009:c18
- **Claim:** Extinction and reconsolidation have distinct biochemical signatures.
- **Quote:** "extinction and reconsolidation have different biochemical signatures."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ0

### nader2009:c19
- **Claim:** In human motor-sequence learning, learning a new sequence after reactivation of an old one causes interference only when the old sequence was reactivated.
- **Quote:** "subjects then learn a new sequence. Testing memory for the old sequence reveals memory interference only when it was reactivated."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

### nader2009:c20
- **Claim:** Reconsolidation in contextual fear conditioning depends on reactivation duration: memories acquired with strong training did not reconsolidate with 3-5 min reactivation but did reconsolidate with 10 min reactivation.
- **Quote:** "memories that were acquired with a strong training protocol of three shocks did not undergo reconsolidation if the reactivation session took 3 or 5 minutes, but reactivating memory for 10 minutes triggered reconsolidation."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

## Extraction Notes

- Full-text extraction from Nature Reviews Neuroscience feature review. 40 claims in raw agent output; condensed to 20 highest-impact for pipeline consumption.
- c5 is the single most architecturally consequential claim for memex: every recall modifies the memory. This means memex's "stored unit" is not a static record but something rewritten on each retrieval event.
- c12 is directly relevant to the fog-match hypothesis: in humans, reconsolidation of episodic memory requires reinstatement of the spatial learning context.
- c4 (PKMzeta maintenance) means even "stable" memories require continuous molecular upkeep — storage is active, not passive.
- The reconsolidation framework directly challenges the Squire 1982 classical consolidation model (also extracted this batch).
