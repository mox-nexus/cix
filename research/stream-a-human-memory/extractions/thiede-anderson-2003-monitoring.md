# Extraction: Thiede et al 2003 — Metacognitive Monitoring Accuracy

## Atomic Claims

### thiede2003:c1
- **Claim:** Metacognitive monitoring accuracy affects regulation of study, which in turn affects overall learning outcomes.
- **Quote:** "Metacognitive monitoring affects regulation of study, and this affects overall learning."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4

### thiede2003:c2
- **Claim:** Generating keywords after a delay (delayed-keyword condition) produced greater monitoring accuracy than generating immediately or not at all.
- **Quote:** "Accuracy was greater for a group that wrote keywords after a delay (delayed-keyword group) than for a group that wrote keywords immediately after reading (immediatekeyword group) and a group that did not write keywords (no-keyword group)."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4

### thiede2003:c3
- **Claim:** Superior monitoring accuracy led to more effective regulation of study and greater overall reading comprehension test performance.
- **Quote:** "The superior monitoring accuracy produced more effective regulation of study. Differences in monitoring accuracy and regulation of study, in turn, produced greater overall test performance (reading comprehension)"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4

### thiede2003:c4
- **Claim:** Delayed keyword generation improves metacognitive accuracy because it forces retrieval from long-term memory rather than short-term, producing better-calibrated judgments.
- **Quote:** (inferred from discrepancy-reduction model context; the delay forces gist-based processing)
- **Tier:** 2 | **Confidence:** Medium | **RQ:** RQ4
