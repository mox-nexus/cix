# Extraction: Stafford & Daly 1984 — Conversational Memory

## Atomic Claims

### stafford1984:c1
- **Claim:** Conversations are distinctly different stimuli from most other things people remember: they are jointly created, constantly updated, and readily accessible by participants.
- **Quote:** "Conversations represent stimuli that are distinctly different in a number of ways from most other stimuli that people are required to remember. Conversations are jointly created, constantly updated, and necessarily are readily accessible by participants."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2
- **Note:** KEY CLAIM for memex. Conversations ARE different from other memory stimuli. Three properties: jointly created, constantly updated, readily accessible. These are design constraints.

### stafford1984:c2
- **Claim:** After only five minutes, people can recollect only about 10% of what was said in a social exchange.
- **Quote:** "even after only five minutes people are able to recollect only about 10% of what was said in a social exchange."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2
- **Note:** ARCHITECTURALLY LOAD-BEARING. 90% of conversation content is lost within 5 minutes. This is the fundamental justification for memex: without external memory, most conversational content vanishes almost immediately.

### stafford1984:c3
- **Claim:** The mode of recall (written vs oral) and the presence and type of memory goals affected what and how much of the interaction was recalled.
- **Quote:** "the mode of recall (written versus oral) and the presence and type of memory goals affected what and how much of the interaction was recalled."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

### stafford1984:c4
- **Claim:** Subjects remembered more of their partner's comments than their own during the interaction.
- **Quote:** "Subjects also remembered more of their partner's comments in the interaction than their own."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ2, RQ3

### stafford1984:c5
- **Claim:** Oral communication anxiety affected both production of discourse and recall of interactions.
- **Quote:** "oral communication anxiety affected both the production of discourse in the conversations and recall of the interactions"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3

### stafford1984:c6

- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ3
