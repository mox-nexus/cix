# Extraction: Metcalfe 1986 — Feeling of Knowing

## Atomic Claims

### metcalfe-1986:c1
- **Claim:** In Experiment 1, participants first made feeling-of-knowing (FOK) judgments about trivia questions they could not answer, then took a multiple-choice recognition test on those questions.
- **Quote:** "In Experiment 1 subjects judged their feelings of knowing to trivia questions they had been unable to answer, then performed a multiple-choice recognition test"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4

### metcalfe-1986:c2
- **Claim:** In Experiment 1, a second task required participants to give FOK judgments for insight problems they could not immediately solve.
- **Quote:** "In a second task, subjects gave feeling-of-knowing judgments for problems to which they did not immediately know the answers."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1, RQ4

### metcalfe-1986:c3
- **Claim:** After making FOK judgments on insight problems, participants were given 5 minutes to attempt to solve each problem.
- **Quote:** "Later, they were given 5 mia to solve each problem."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1
- **Note:** "5 mia" is an OCR artifact for "5 min."

### metcalfe-1986:c4
- **Claim:** In Experiment 1, FOK judgments for trivia questions showed a positive correlation with subsequent recognition test performance.
- **Quote:** "In contrast to the positive correlation found in the memory task"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4
- **Note:** No effect size reported in abstract. Direction is stated; magnitude unknown.

### metcalfe-1986:c5
- **Claim:** In Experiment 1, participants' FOK rank ordering of insight problems showed no relationship to whether those problems were subsequently solved.
- **Quote:** "the feeling-of-knowing rank ordering of insight problems did not relate to problem solution."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4, RQ1

### metcalfe-1986:c6
- **Claim:** Experiment 2 replicated Experiment 1 using a generation memory technique in place of the multiple-choice recognition test.
- **Quote:** "Experiment 2 provided a replication of Experiment 1 with a generation memory technique rather than a multiplechoice recognition test."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4
- **Note:** "multiplechoice" is an OCR artifact for "multiple-choice."

### metcalfe-1986:c7
- **Claim:** Across both experiments, participants could predict their own memory performance with reasonable accuracy.
- **Quote:** "Both experiments showed that although people could predict memory performance reasonably well"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4

### metcalfe-1986:c8
- **Claim:** Across both experiments, participants' FOK judgments for insight problems showed zero predictive validity for whether those problems would be solved.
- **Quote:** "predictive metacognitions were nonexistent for the problems."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ4, RQ1

### metcalfe-1986:c9
- **Claim:** Metcalfe (1986) interprets the findings as implying that insight problem solving involves a sudden illumination event.
- **Quote:** "The data are interpreted as implying that insight problems do involve a sudden illumination"
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** Medium | **RQ:** RQ1
- **Note:** Hedged — "interpreted as implying." Inference from null FOK-solution correlation, not direct measurement of illumination.

### metcalfe-1986:c10
- **Claim:** Insight illumination cannot be predicted in advance, as demonstrated by the absence of predictive validity in FOK judgments for insight problems across two experiments.
- **Quote:** "that illumination cannot be predicted in advance."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** Medium | **RQ:** RQ1, RQ4
- **Note:** Load-bearing for memex: if illumination cannot be predicted, memex cannot use pre-click FOK signals to flag imminent insights. Detection must be post-hoc or via behavioral proxies.

## Extraction Notes

- This paper is a confirmed DOUBLE HIT on RQ1 and RQ4. The empirical findings (c4, c5, c7, c8) are primarily RQ4 evidence (metacognition reliability), but they bear on RQ1 through the interpretive layer (c9, c10 — what insight IS as a cognitive event).
- The central dissociation: FOK predicts memory (c4, c7) but NOT insight (c5, c8). This replicates across recognition and generation memory tests (c6).
- c9 and c10 are set to Medium confidence because they add one inference step beyond the measurement. "Sudden illumination" is a theoretical construct inferred from the absence of metacognitive forewarning.
- OCR typos preserved verbatim in quotes, flagged in notes: "5 mia" (c3), "multiplechoice" (c6).
- Abstract-only limitation: gamma correlations, sample sizes, specific insight problems used, and statistical test details are in the full paper. Flag for full-text retrieval.
