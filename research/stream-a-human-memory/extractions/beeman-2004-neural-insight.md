# Extraction: Beeman et al 2004 — Neural Activity and Insight

## Atomic Claims

### beeman2004:c1
- **Claim:** fMRI revealed increased activity in the right hemisphere anterior superior temporal gyrus (RH aSTG) for insight relative to noninsight solutions.
- **Quote:** "Functional magnetic resonance imaging (Experiment 1) revealed increased activity in the right hemisphere anterior superior temporal gyrus for insight relative to noninsight solutions."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1

### beeman2004:c2
- **Claim:** EEG revealed a sudden burst of gamma-band neural activity in the same RH aSTG area beginning 0.3 seconds prior to insight solutions.
- **Quote:** "Scalp electroencephalogram recordings (Experiment 2) revealed a sudden burst of high-frequency (gamma-band) neural activity in the same area beginning 0.3 s prior to insight solutions."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1
- **Note:** THE neural signature of insight. Gamma burst 300ms before the click. This is the measurable precursor Kounios & Beeman 2009 synthesized.

### beeman2004:c3
- **Claim:** The right anterior temporal area involved in insight is associated with making connections across distantly related information during comprehension.
- **Quote:** "This right anterior temporal area is associated with making connections across distantly related information during comprehension."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1

### beeman2004:c4
- **Claim:** All problem solving relies on a largely shared cortical network; the insight-specific difference is engagement of distinct neural processes allowing connections that previously eluded the solver.
- **Quote:** "Although all problem solving relies on a largely shared cortical network, the sudden flash of insight occurs when solvers engage distinct neural and cognitive processes that allow them to see connections that previously eluded them."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1

### beeman2004:c5
- **Claim:** Subjects self-reported whether they solved with or without insight after each correct solution; this self-report correlated with objective neural differences.
- **Quote:** "after each correct solution indicated whether they solved with or without insight. We observed two objective neural correlates of insight."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1, RQ4
- **Note:** Self-report of insight IS reliable enough to sort trials into neurally distinct categories. This is the COUNTER to Metcalfe's "no privileged access" — people CAN report THAT insight happened (post-hoc), even if they can't PREDICT it (pre-hoc).

### beeman2004:c6
- **Claim:** The RH aSTG region active during insight was also active during initial solving efforts, suggesting preparatory processing in the same region that later produces the insight.
- **Quote:** "The same region was active during initial solving efforts."
- **Tier:** 1 | **Confidence:** High | **RQ:** RQ1
