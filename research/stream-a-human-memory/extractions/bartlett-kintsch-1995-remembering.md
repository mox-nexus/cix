## Extraction Notes

- Source material is a publisher abstract ("about the book" summary for the 1995 reissue), not the book's full text. Claims are what the publisher summary asserts about the book's arguments.
- c6 (reconstruction) and c7 (social act) split from one sentence: "Bartlett developed his claim that memory is a process of reconstruction, and that this construction is in important ways a social act." These are two distinct theoretical commitments — one epistemological (memory is reconstructive), one sociological (reconstruction is social).
- c6 is the load-bearing claim for RQ2: it is Bartlett's direct rebuttal to the "memory as faithful recording" model, which is the RQ2 null hypothesis.
- Full-text extraction of the book would yield substantially more, particularly around specific schema mechanisms, the range of experiments beyond "War of the Ghosts," and the theoretical apparatus distinguishing schema-driven reconstruction from simple forgetting.
