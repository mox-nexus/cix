# Sources and Bibliography

Research informing the craft-explanations plugin design.

## Pedagogy and Learning Science

- Bastani, H., Bastani, O., Sungu, A., Ge, H., KabakcÄ±, O., & Mariman, R. (2025). Generative AI without guardrails can harm learning. *PNAS*.
  - GPT Tutor (hints only): no harm. GPT Base (direct answers): 17% harm.

- Tetzlaff, L. et al. (2025). Expertise Reversal Effect: A meta-analysis. n=5,924.
  - High guidance helps novices (d=0.505), harms experts (d=-0.428).

- Demszky, D. et al. (2025). Feynman Bot: Learning gains from AI tutoring. Stanford.
  - 1.8x learning gains from example-first AI tutoring.

- Mayer, R. (2009). *Multimedia Learning*. Cambridge University Press.
  - Coherence principle: d=0.86 across 23/23 tests. Extraneous material harms learning.

- Sweller, J. (1988). Cognitive load during problem solving. *Cognitive Science*.
  - Three types: intrinsic, extraneous, germane. Minimize extraneous, protect germane.

## Explanation Science

- Miller, T. (2019). Explanation in Artificial Intelligence: Insights from the Social Sciences. *Artificial Intelligence*, 267, 1-38.
  - Explanations are contrastive ("Why X, not Y?"), social, and selective.

- Lombrozo, T. (2012). Explanation and abductive inference. *Oxford Handbook of Thinking and Reasoning*.
  - Explanatory preferences shape learning and generalization.

## AI and Prompting

- arXiv:2510.22251. Prompting Inversion for frontier models.
  - Explicit procedural constraints hurt frontier models. Route, don't teach. WHY framing: 31.5% to 97.3%.

- Dhuliawala, S. et al. (2024). Chain-of-Verification Reduces Hallucination in Large Language Models. *ACL*.
  - Independent verification achieves 50-70% hallucination reduction. +23% accuracy.

## Cognitive Science

- Lee, J. et al. (2025). The Impact of Generative AI on Critical Thinking. *CHI*.
  - AI confidence negatively predicts critical thinking (beta = -0.69).

- Kosmyna, N. et al. (2025). Your Brain on ChatGPT: Cognitive Debt. MIT Media Lab.
  - Neural connectivity "systematically scaled down" with AI use (EEG study).

## Reading and Information Design

- Nielsen, J. (2006). F-Shaped Pattern For Reading Web Content. Nielsen Norman Group.
  - 232 users eye-tracked. First paragraphs get most attention. Headers as signposts.

- Pernice, K. (2017). F-Shaped Pattern: Misunderstood, But Still Relevant. NN/g.

- Tufte, E. (1983). *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*. Graphics Press.
  - Data-ink ratio. Every visual element should carry information.

## Information Architecture

- Rosenfeld, L., Morville, P., & Arango, J. (2015). *Information Architecture: For the Web and Beyond* (4th ed.). O'Reilly.

- Procida, D. (2017). Diataxis: A systematic framework for technical documentation.
  - Tutorial / How-to / Explanation / Reference. Don't mix types.

## AI Writing

- Wikipedia: Signs of AI Writing.
  - Vocabulary tells: delve, leverage, robust. Structural tells: uniform paragraphs, rule-of-three.

- Shankar, S. (2025). Writing in the Age of LLMs.
  - Stylometric analysis shows LLM text clusters tightly vs human variation.

- arXiv:2509.19163. Measuring AI "Slop" in Text.

## Philosophy

- Bhagavad Gita / Samkhya. Three gunas: Sattva, Rajas, Tamas.
  - Not quality levels. Dimensions of perception. All present simultaneously.

- Marr, D. (1982). *Vision*. Computational, algorithmic, implementational levels.
  - Parallel structure: principle (computational), pattern (algorithmic), practice (implementational).
