## Skill Formation & Learning

- Shen, J.H. & Tamkin, A. (2026). [How AI Impacts Skill Formation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.20245). Anthropic. Cohen's d = 0.738 (17pp skill gap). → [skill-formation](../explanation/skill-formation.md), [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Bastani, H. et al. (2025). [Generative AI Can Harm Learning](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2422633122). PNAS. -17% without guardrails, no harm with guardrails. → [problem](../explanation/the-problem.md), [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Zhou, Y. et al. (2025). [Creative Scar Without Generative AI](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0160791X25002775). Technology in Society. Longitudinal: creativity drops on withdrawal, homogeneity persists months later. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)

## Cognitive Effects

- Lee, H.P. et al. (2025). [The Impact of Generative AI on Critical Thinking](https://dl.acm.org/doi/full/10.1145/3706598.3713778). CHI 2025. β = -0.69 (AI confidence → less CT). → [problem](../explanation/the-problem.md), [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Kosmyna, N. et al. (2025). [Your Brain on ChatGPT](https://www.media.mit.edu/publications/your-brain-on-chatgpt/). MIT Media Lab. 83% recall failure, reduced brain connectivity. → [problem](../explanation/the-problem.md), [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Gerlich, M. (2025). [AI Tools in Society](https://www.mdpi.com/2075-4698/15/1/6). MDPI Societies. r = -0.68 (AI ↔ CT), r = -0.75 (offloading ↔ CT). → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Bansal, G. et al. (2021). Does the Whole Exceed its Parts? CHI 2021. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Sharma, M. et al. (2026). [Who's in Charge? Disempowerment Patterns](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.19062). Anthropic. 1.5M conversations analyzed. Users rate harmful interactions favorably. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)

## Homogenization & Diversity

- Jiang, L. et al. (2025). [Artificial Hivemind: Open-Ended Homogeneity of LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.22954). **NeurIPS 2025 Best Paper**. 70+ LLMs converge; temperature/ensembling don't help. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md), [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Doshi, A.R. & Hauser, O.P. (2024). [Individual Creativity vs Collective Diversity](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adn5290). Science Advances. +8% individual novelty, +10.7% story similarity. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md), [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Meta-analysis (2025). [Generative AI and Creativity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.17241). arXiv. **g = -0.863** diversity reduction (28 studies, n=8,214). → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Hintze, A. et al. (2026). [Visual Elevator Music](https://www.cell.com/patterns/fulltext/S2666-3899(25)00299-5). Patterns/Cell. 700 AI image runs → 12 motifs. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Ashery, A.F. et al. (2025). [Emergent Collective Bias in LLM Populations](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/sciadv.adu9368). Science Advances. Bias emerges between agents, not within. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Agarwal, D. et al. (2025). [AI Homogenizes Writing Toward Western Styles](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706598.3713564). CHI 2025. 7.1pp cultural distinctiveness reduction. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Shumailov, I. et al. (2024). [Model Collapse](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07566-y). Nature. AI trained on AI output loses tails, then collapses. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md)
- Xu et al. (2025). Echoes in AI: LLM Homogenization. PNAS. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md), [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Zhang et al. (2025). [AI and Survey Response Homogenization](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00491241251327130). Sociological Methods & Research. 34% used LLMs; responses more homogeneous. → [evidence](../explanation/the-evidence.md), [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Hong & Page (2004). Groups of Diverse Problem Solvers. PNAS. → [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Lorenz et al. (2011). How Social Influence Undermines Crowd Wisdom. PNAS.
- Jeppesen & Lakhani (2010). Marginality and Problem-Solving. Organization Science.
- Janis, I. (1972/1982). Groupthink.
- Surowiecki, J. (2004). The Wisdom of Crowds.
- Phillips, K. et al. (2006-2014). How Diversity Works.
- Ashby, W.R. (1956). Law of Requisite Variety. → [first-principles](../explanation/first-principles-ci.md), [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
- Haldane, A. (2009, 2016). Financial Monoculture. Bank of England. → [why-it-matters](../explanation/why-it-matters.md)
