# The Evidence

AI reliably improves immediate task performance while simultaneously degrading the cognitive foundations that enable long-term capability. Both findings are real, and the interaction pattern — not the technology — determines which outcome you get. This library synthesizes the research from premier venues (CHI, PNAS, Nature, NeurIPS), with full citations and explicit marking where the argument is assembled from convergent evidence rather than directly demonstrated. Start from a reading path below, or read in order.

1. **[The Paradox](explanation/the-paradox)** — AI reliably improves immediate task performance while simultaneously degrading the cognitive foundations that enable long-term capability.
2. **[The Mechanism](explanation/the-mechanism)** — The generative step — the cognitive work of constructing understanding — is what AI bypasses, and it is where learning happens.
3. **[The Design Lever](explanation/the-design-lever)** — Same tool, different engagement model, opposite outcome: process control and mastery orientation are the measured levers.
4. **[The Stakes](explanation/the-stakes)** — Substitutive AI use produces measurably less diverse output at the individual level and risks model collapse at the systemic level.
5. **[What cix Does](explanation/what-cix-does)** — How cix operationalizes the design levers — process control at install, transparency through dual content, mastery through orthogonality.
6. **[Honest Limits](explanation/honest-limits)** — What the research has not measured, what claims are assembled rather than demonstrated, and where cix itself remains a bet.
